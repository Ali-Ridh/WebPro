Discover Ampel

Ampel is the heart of religious and cultural heritage in Surabaya. Visit Pasar Ampel and immerse yourself in the Arab and Madurese culture, surrounded by historical mosques and vibrant market life.

Expect a blend of Middle Eastern and Javanese cultures, and an ambiance filled with the sounds, smells, and sights of history and devotion.

Tugu Pahlawan

A symbol of Surabaya's fight for independence, Tugu Pahlawan stands tall as a reminder of the bravery of the city's people. The legendary Bung Tomo and the Surabaya youth played a vital role in the battle against colonial forces here.

Tunjungan: A Blend of History & Modernity

Tunjungan is where history meets modernity. Famous for the tearing of the Netherlands flag at Hotel Yamato, Tunjungan is now home to great culinary spots and shopping experiences at Tunjungan Mall.

Don’t miss Pasar Tunjungan and my personal recommendation: Kokoro Kubari, a cozy Japanese food haven in Surabaya.

Ampel - A Cultural Journey

Ampel has been a cultural and religious center since the 15th century. Explore its vibrant Arab quarter, with its traditional markets and religious significance tied to the Ampel Mosque.

Tugu Pahlawan - Heroic Struggle

Tugu Pahlawan is a memorial to the Battle of Surabaya in November 1945. It commemorates the day when Bung Tomo and the youth of Surabaya stood up against colonial forces, marking a pivotal moment in Indonesia’s fight for independence.

Tunjungan - A Historical & Culinary Experience

At Hotel Yamato, Surabaya's youth tore the blue section off the Dutch flag, turning it into the Indonesian red-and-white flag. Today, Tunjungan is known for its great food and shopping spots. Make sure to visit Kokoro Kubari, a cozy spot for Japanese cuisine, and explore the vibrant Pasar Tunjungan.
